Final Project Report - Purrfect Timing

The following report documents the features that I have implemented for my final project of the course Computer Graphics. The given theme this year is "Perfect Timing".

Motivation

There is so much happening around us everyday, all seemingly completely random, affected by so many random variables. But, sometimes, almost as if we were in a film, there are things that seem to happen with the perfect timing. My composition wishes to convey a chance encounter, seemingly an act of fate, that leads to the start of a new relation. This is represented by two felines that happen to cross each others' paths admist a busy world, capturing the moment where their tails intertwine to form the shape of a heart in a moment of stillness. Whether it be timing, angle, or focus, this chance encounter requires an absolutely... Purrfect timing.

My proposed motivational image is composed of two parts: a background and a foreground. The background is a street, wet from a recent rainfall, and has many lights reflected from the wet surfaces in the scene. The foreground contains two cats, who walk by the middle of the street with a perfect timing such that their tails intertwine to form the shape of a heart. The background, together with the foreground, aim to set up an atmosphere of refreshing calm admist a busy world, a moment where despite the busyness, time stops for a moment. Features

The following list describes the features I would like to implement:

  • Simple extra emitter (5 pts) - Create a spotlight for lampposts in the streets. Validation via comparison with the Mitsuba implementation of the spotlight.
  • Layered BSDF (15 pts) - Rain on cement, to allow reflective lights on the ground. Validation via comparison with the Mitsuba implementation of internal scattering (under plastic BSDF).
  • Fur Rendering (40 pts) - Render the fur of the cats in my scene. A full model includes curve primitives, an acceleration structure like BVH or KD-Tree, and Marschner hair BSDF. Validation will be done by comparing the rendered image with the implementation on a mature renderer like PBRT or Maya.

I will also need to use Blender to modify and piece together available meshes according to the scene I described above.
